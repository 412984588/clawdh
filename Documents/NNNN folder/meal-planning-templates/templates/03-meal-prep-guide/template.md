# Meal Prep Guide

Cook once, eat all week. A 2–3 hour Sunday session saves 5+ hours of weeknight cooking.

## Meal Prep Sunday — Master Schedule

**Prep date:** _______________
**Meals to prep:** ___ breakfasts + ___ lunches + ___ dinners + ___ snacks

### Phase 1: Setup (15 min)
- [ ] Clear counter space and clean kitchen
- [ ] Set out all containers (glass preferred — no microwave plastic)
- [ ] Preheat oven to 400°F / 200°C
- [ ] Pull out all recipes and ingredients

### Phase 2: Start Long-Cook Items (10 min hands-on → cook while you prep)
- [ ] Put rice/grains in rice cooker or pot
- [ ] Start slow cooker protein (if applicable)
- [ ] Season and place sheet pan items in oven

### Phase 3: Chop Everything (30 min)
- [ ] Wash and chop all vegetables
- [ ] Dice onions, mince garlic
- [ ] Slice fruits for snacks
- [ ] Portion salad greens into containers

### Phase 4: Cook Proteins (30 min)
- [ ] Grill or bake chicken breasts/thighs
- [ ] Brown ground meat
- [ ] Cook eggs (hard-boiled, 12 at a time)
- [ ] Prepare tofu/tempeh (if applicable)

### Phase 5: Assemble (30 min)
- [ ] Portion meals into containers
- [ ] Label with name + date
- [ ] Store: fridge (eat within 4 days) or freezer (eat within 3 months)

### Phase 6: Clean (15 min)
- [ ] Wash dishes, wipe counters
- [ ] Take stock of what's prepped
- [ ] Update Pantry Inventory (template 10)

**Total time: ~2–2.5 hours**

## The Mix-and-Match Formula

Build any meal from these categories:

### Proteins (pick 2 per week)
| Protein | Prep Method | Prep Time | Servings | Cost/Serving |
|---------|-----------|----------|---------|-------------|
| Chicken breast | Bake at 400°F, 25 min | 5 min | 4 | ~$1.50 |

| Ground beef/turkey | Brown in skillet, 10 min | 5 min | 4 | ~$1.50 |

## Food Safety Rules

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Fridge temp | Keep at 40°F / 4°C or below |
| Eat prepped meals within | 4 days (fridge) or 3 months (freezer) |
| Cool before storing | Let food cool 30 min before sealing containers |
| Reheat to | 165°F / 74°C (use a food thermometer) |
| Never refreeze | Thawed food should not be refrozen |
