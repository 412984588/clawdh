# Seasonal Meal Planner

Eat what's in season — it's cheaper, tastier, and more nutritious. This guide covers all four seasons.

## Why Eat Seasonally?

| Benefit | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **30–50% cheaper** | In-season produce doesn't need importing or greenhouse growing |
| **Better taste** | Picked ripe, not shipped unripe from another continent |
| **More nutritious** | Fresh-picked produce has higher vitamin content |
| **Supports local farms** | Seasonal = usually local at farmers markets |
| **Meal variety** | Forces natural rotation so you don't eat the same thing year-round |

## Seasonal Produce Guide

### Spring (March–May)

**Peak produce:**
| Vegetable | Best Recipes | Storage |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| Asparagus | Roasted, grilled, in pasta, in omelettes | Fridge 3–5 days |
| Peas (snap, snow) | Stir-fry, salads, pasta, eaten raw | Fridge 3–5 days |
| Artichokes | Steamed with butter, roasted, in dips | Fridge 5–7 days |
| Radishes | Salads, roasted, with butter and salt | Fridge 1–2 weeks |
| Spinach | Salads, smoothies, sautéed, in eggs | Fridge 5–7 days |
| Strawberries | Fresh, in smoothies, on oats, desserts | Fridge 3–5 days |
| Rhubarb | Pies, jams, compotes | Fridge 1–2 weeks |

**Spring dinner ideas:**
1. Asparagus and pea risotto
2. Spring vegetable pasta with lemon
3. Strawberry spinach salad with grilled chicken
4. Snap pea stir-fry with shrimp

---

### Summer (June–August)

**Peak produce:**
| Vegetable/Fruit | Best Recipes | Storage |
|----------------|-------------|---------|
| Tomatoes | Caprese, BLT, salsa, pasta sauce, gazpacho | Counter (never fridge) |
| Zucchini | Grilled, in pasta, zucchini bread, stir-fry | Fridge 5–7 days |
| Corn | Grilled, boiled, in salads, corn chowder | Fridge 1–3 days (best fresh) |
| Bell peppers | Raw, roasted, stuffed, stir-fry | Fridge 1–2 weeks |
| Cucumbers | Salads, pickles, tzatziki, eaten raw | Fridge 1 week |
| Berries (all) | Fresh, smoothies, pies, jams, on oats | Fridge 3–5 days |
| Peaches | Fresh, grilled, cobbler, smoothies | Counter then fridge |
| Watermelon | Fresh, in salads, agua fresca | Fridge 3–5 days (cut) |

**Summer dinner ideas:**
1. Grilled chicken with corn and tomato salad
2. Stuffed bell peppers with rice and ground turkey
3. Zucchini noodles with pesto and cherry tomatoes
4. Grilled peach and burrata salad
5. Fish tacos with mango salsa

---

### Fall (September–November)

**Peak produce:**
| Vegetable/Fruit | Best Recipes | Storage |
|----------------|-------------|---------|
| Butternut squash | Roasted, in soup, ravioli filling, mashed | Cool dry place, 2–3 months |
| Sweet potatoes | Baked, mashed, in chili, casserole | Cool dry place, 2–4 weeks |
| Apples | Fresh, pie, sauce, butter, baked | Fridge 4–6 weeks |
| Pumpkin | Soup, pie, roasted seeds, bread | Cool dry place, 2–3 months |
| Brussels sprouts | Roasted, shaved in salads, with bacon | Fridge 1–2 weeks |
| Cauliflower | Roasted, mashed, in curry, as rice | Fridge 1 week |
| Pears | Fresh, poached, in salads, with cheese | Counter then fridge |
| Cranberries | Sauce, baked goods, dried in salads | Fridge 3–4 weeks |

**Fall dinner ideas:**
1. Butternut squash soup with crusty bread
2. Apple pork chops with mashed sweet potatoes
3. Roasted Brussels sprouts and sausage sheet pan
4. Pumpkin chili
5. Cauliflower curry with rice

---

### Winter (December–February)

**Peak produce:**
| Vegetable/Fruit | Best Recipes | Storage |
|----------------|-------------|---------|
| Citrus (oranges, grapefruit, lemon) | Fresh, juice, salad dressing, baking | Counter 1 week, fridge 3 weeks |
| Kale | Salads, smoothies, soup, chips | Fridge 5–7 days |
| Cabbage | Coleslaw, stir-fry, braised, soup | Fridge 1–2 months |
| Root veggies (turnips, parsnips, beets) | Roasted, in stews, mashed | Fridge 2–3 weeks |
| Potatoes | Mashed, baked, in stews, hash | Cool dark place, 2–3 months |
| Leeks | Soup, gratin, braised | Fridge 1–2 weeks |
| Winter squash (acorn, spaghetti) | Roasted, stuffed, in soup | Cool dry place, 1–3 months |

**Winter dinner ideas:**
1. Beef stew with root vegetables
2. Kale and white bean soup
3. Braised cabbage with sausage
4. Citrus-glazed salmon with roasted beets
5. Loaded baked potato soup

## Preserving the Season

| Method | Best For | Keeps For | Effort |
|--------|---------|----------|--------|
| Freezing | Berries, corn, tomato sauce, pesto | 6–12 months | Low |
| Canning | Tomatoes, pickles, jams, salsa | 12+ months | Medium |
| Drying | Herbs, tomatoes, fruit leather | 6–12 months | Low |
| Fermenting | Cabbage (sauerkraut), cucumbers (pickles) | Months | Medium |
