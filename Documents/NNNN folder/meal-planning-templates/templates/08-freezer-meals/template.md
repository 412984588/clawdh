# Freezer Meals Guide

Cook once, freeze, eat for weeks. The ultimate time-saving meal system.

## Freezer Meal Basics

**How long food lasts in the freezer:**
| Food Type | Freezer Life | Best Container |
|-----------|-------------|---------------|
| Cooked meat (chicken, beef) | 3–4 months | Freezer bags or containers |
| Soups and stews | 4–6 months | Mason jars (leave headspace) or bags |
| Casseroles | 3–4 months | Foil pans (freeze → oven ready) |
| Meatballs | 3–4 months | Sheet pan freeze, then bag |
| Burritos/wraps | 3 months | Wrap in foil, then bag |
| Pancakes/waffles | 2–3 months | Separate with parchment |
| Sauces (marinara, pesto) | 6 months | Ice cube trays → bags |
| Cookie dough | 3 months | Scoop onto sheet pan, freeze, then bag |

## Freezer Cooking Day Plan (4 Hours → 30+ Meals)

### What You'll Make
Choose 4–5 recipes from the list above to cook in one session.

### Schedule

**Hour 1: Prep**
- [ ] Defrost any needed ingredients
- [ ] Chop all onions, garlic, vegetables at once
- [ ] Brown all ground meat at once (use 2 pans if needed)
- [ ] Set slow cooker going if making pulled pork/stew

**Hour 2: Cook Batch 1**
- [ ] Start soup on stovetop
- [ ] Assemble and bake casserole #1
- [ ] Roll meatballs and place on sheet pan in oven

**Hour 3: Cook Batch 2**
- [ ] Start second recipe on stovetop
- [ ] Assemble enchiladas or burritos
- [ ] Begin cooling completed recipes

**Hour 4: Package and Label**
- [ ] Cool everything to room temperature
- [ ] Portion into labeled containers/bags
- [ ] Label with: Recipe name, date, servings, reheat instructions
- [ ] Arrange in freezer (oldest in front)
- [ ] Clean kitchen

## Freezing Tips

| Tip | Why |
|-----|-----|
| Cool food completely before freezing | Warm food raises freezer temp, causes ice crystals |
| Remove air from bags | Air causes freezer burn |
| Freeze flat (bags on a sheet pan) | Stacks neatly, thaws faster |
| Leave 1 inch headspace in containers | Liquid expands when frozen |
| Label everything: name + date + reheat | You will forget what's in that container |
| Use within 3–4 months | Quality declines after that |
| Thaw in fridge overnight | Safest method; microwave for quick thaw |

## What NOT to Freeze

| Food | Why |
|------|-----|
| Cream-based sauces | Separate and become grainy |
| Lettuce/raw greens | Turn to mush |
| Cooked pasta (in sauce is OK) | Gets mushy; slightly undercook if freezing |
| Fried foods | Lose crispness (reheat in oven to partially recover) |
| Eggs in shell | Expand and crack |
| Mayo-based salads | Separate badly |
