## Where to Keep Your Emergency Fund

| Option | Interest Rate | Access Speed | FDIC Insured? | Best For |
|--------|-------------|-------------|--------------|---------|
| High-yield savings account | 4–5% APY | 1–2 business days | Yes | Best overall choice |
| Money market account | 4–5% APY | Same day (with debit) | Yes | Quick access |
| Regular savings account | 0.01–0.5% APY | Instant | Yes | Avoid — too low |
| Under the mattress | 0% | Instant | No | Avoid — loses to inflation |
| CDs (certificates of deposit) | 4–5% APY | Penalty for early withdrawal | Yes | Only for excess beyond 6 months |

**Recommended:** High-yield savings account at an online bank (Marcus, Ally, Discover, Wealthfront).

## Emergency Fund Rules

1. **This money is for emergencies only** — not sales, not vacations, not "I want it"
2. **What counts as an emergency:**
   - Job loss or income reduction
   - Medical emergency or unexpected health costs
   - Essential car or home repair
   - Urgent family situation
3. **What does NOT count:**
   - Holiday shopping
   - Vacation
   - New phone
   - "Good deal" on something
4. **If you use it, replenish it** — Treat replenishment as a top priority
