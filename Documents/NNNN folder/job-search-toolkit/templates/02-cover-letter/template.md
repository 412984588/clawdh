# Cover Letter Templates

Three proven cover letter structures. Pick the one that fits, fill in, and customize per role.

## Template 1: The Classic (Most Roles)

```
Dear [Hiring Manager Name / "Hiring Team"],

I'm writing to apply for the [Job Title] position at [Company Name].
With [X] years of experience in [relevant field], I'm excited about the
opportunity to [what you'd contribute to this specific company].

In my current role at [Current Company], I [achievement #1 with numbers].
I also [achievement #2 with numbers]. These experiences have prepared me
to [what the job requires, using language from the job posting].

What draws me to [Company Name] specifically is [genuine reason — product,
mission, recent news, culture]. I believe my background in [your strength]
aligns well with your team's focus on [their priority from the job posting].

I'd welcome the chance to discuss how I can contribute to [specific goal
or project]. I'm available for an interview at your convenience.

Best regards,
[Your Name]
[Phone] | [Email] | [LinkedIn]
```

## Template 2: The Storyteller (Creative / Startup Roles)

```
Dear [Name],

[Open with a 1–2 sentence story or hook that connects you to the role.]

That experience taught me [lesson], and it's exactly why the [Job Title]
role at [Company] caught my attention.

Over the past [X] years, I've:
- [Achievement with numbers]
- [Achievement with numbers]
- [Achievement with numbers]

What excites me about [Company] is [specific thing — product, mission,
recent milestone]. I want to bring my experience in [your skill] to help
[their goal].

I'd love to tell you more about how [specific thing you can do for them].
Let's connect.

[Your Name]
[Contact info]
```

## Template 3: The Value Proposition (Sales / Business Roles)

```
Dear [Name],

You need someone who can [core requirement from job posting]. I've done
exactly that.

At [Company], I [achievement #1 with hard numbers]. Before that, at
[Company], I [achievement #2 with hard numbers].

Here's what I'd bring to [Company Name] in the first 90 days:

1. [Specific action you'd take based on their challenges]
2. [Specific improvement you'd drive based on your experience]
3. [Specific initiative you'd launch]

I've researched [Company's] [product/market/recent news], and I see
an opportunity to [insight that shows you've done your homework].

Let's schedule 20 minutes to discuss. I'm available [days/times].

[Your Name]
[Contact info]
```

## Cover Letter Rules

| Rule | Why |
|------|-----|
| Max 1 page, 3–4 paragraphs | Hiring managers spend <30 seconds scanning |
| Address someone by name | "Dear Hiring Manager" is forgettable |
| Don't repeat your resume | Add context and personality that the resume can't |
| Show you researched the company | Generic = rejected |
| Include numbers | "Increased revenue 23%" beats "helped grow revenue" |
| Match their language | Use terms from the job posting naturally |
| End with a clear ask | Request an interview, suggest next steps |

## How to Find the Hiring Manager's Name

1. Check the job posting for a name
2. Search LinkedIn: "[Company] + [Department] + Manager/Director"
3. Check the company's team page
4. Call the company and ask: "Who's the hiring manager for the [Title] role?"
5. Last resort: "Dear [Department] Team" or "Dear Hiring Team"

## Cover Letter Customization Checklist

- [ ] Company name is correct (not from a previous application)
- [ ] Job title matches exactly
- [ ] Referenced something specific about the company
- [ ] Used 2–3 keywords from the job posting
- [ ] Included 2+ quantified achievements
- [ ] Under 1 page
- [ ] Proofread for typos
- [ ] Saved as "[YourName]_CoverLetter_[Company].pdf"
