## The Negotiation Framework

### Step 1: Receive the Offer (Don't React)

**What to say:**
> "Thank you so much — I'm really excited about this opportunity. I'd love to take a day to review the full offer details. Can you send it in writing?"

**Never accept or reject on the spot.** Even if you love it. Sleep on it.

---

### Step 2: Evaluate the Full Package

| Signing bonus | $ | $___–$___ | $ | Yes |

| Equity / RSUs / options | $ | Varies | $ | Yes |
| PTO / vacation days | ___ days | ___–___ days | ___ days | Sometimes |

**Total compensation (TC):**
```
TC = Base + Annual Bonus + (Equity ÷ Vesting Years) + Benefits Value
```

---

### Step 3: Make Your Counter-Offer

**Script (email or call):**

> "I'm thrilled about joining [Company] as [Title]. After researching market
> rates for this role in [location] and reflecting on my [X years of
> experience / specific skills], I'd like to discuss the base salary.
>
> Based on my research and the value I'd bring — specifically [1–2 concrete
> things you'd deliver] — I was hoping for a base of $[your target].
>
> I'm flexible and want to find something that works for both of us.
> Would that be possible?"

**Rules:**
- Name a specific number (not a range — they'll pick the bottom)
- Aim 10–15% above the offer (gives room to meet in the middle)
- Justify with market data AND your specific value
- Be warm and collaborative, not adversarial

---

### Step 4: Handle Pushback

| They Say | You Say |
|----------|---------|
| "The budget is fixed" | "I understand. Are there other parts of the offer we could adjust? Signing bonus, extra PTO, or earlier review?" |
| "We pay everyone at this level the same" | "I appreciate the consistency. Could we discuss a signing bonus or performance review at 6 months instead of 12?" |
| "This is our best offer" | "I appreciate that. If the base is firm, could we discuss [equity/bonus/PTO/remote days/title]?" |
| "We need an answer by tomorrow" | "I want to give this the consideration it deserves. Could I have until [2–3 days]?" |

---

### Step 5: Get It in Writing

Once you agree verbally:
- [ ] Request updated offer letter with all negotiated terms
- [ ] Review every detail before signing
- [ ] Keep a copy for your records

## What to Negotiate Beyond Salary

| Item | Why It Matters | How to Ask |
|------|--------------|-----------|
| Signing bonus | One-time cost for employer (easier to get) | "Would a signing bonus be possible to bridge the gap?" |
| Remote days | Saves commute time and money | "Could we discuss a hybrid schedule of [X] remote days?" |
| PTO | More rest = better performance | "Would an additional week of PTO be possible?" |
| Title bump | Affects future salary negotiations | "Given my experience, would [Senior Title] be appropriate?" |
| Earlier performance review | Faster path to a raise | "Could we schedule a 6-month review instead of annual?" |
| Professional development budget | Invests in your growth | "Does the team have a learning/conference budget?" |
| Start date | Give yourself breathing room | "Could I start on [date 2 weeks later]?" |

---

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Why It Hurts |
|---------|-------------|
| Accepting the first offer | You leave money on the table — employers expect negotiation |
| Giving a range | They'll always choose the bottom |
| Apologizing for negotiating | Negotiating is normal and expected |
| Threatening to leave | Adversarial tone kills goodwill |
| Not getting it in writing | Verbal agreements aren't enforceable |
| Only negotiating salary | Total comp matters more than base |
| Lying about competing offers | If caught, the offer is rescinded |
