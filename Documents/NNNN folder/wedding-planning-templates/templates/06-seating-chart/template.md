## Seating Strategy

### Priority by Distance from Bride and Groom

| Table Position | Who Sits There |
|---------------|---------------|
| Closest | Immediate family (parents, siblings) |
| Next ring | Extended family (grandparents, aunts, uncles) |
| Middle | Wedding party guests, close friends |
| Outer | Work colleagues, acquaintances |

### Seating Rules

| Rule | Reason |
|------|--------|
| Couples sit together | Never separate partners |
| Feuding family on opposite sides | Prevent ceremony or reception drama |
| Elderly near exits | Easy bathroom access |
| Families with kids near exits | Quick escape for fussy children |
| Single friends grouped together | They'll have more fun meeting each other |
| High-conflict pairs separated | Keep high-conflict guests apart for smooth reception |
| MC positioned near speaker system | So they can manage cake cutting and speeches smoothly |
| Vendor meals at a service table | Reserve seats if venue includes staff meals |

## Ceremony Seating (If Different from Reception)

| Section | Rows | Who |
|---------|------|-----|
| Front left (bride's side) | 1–2 | Immediate family |
| Front right (groom's side) | 1–2 | Immediate family |
| Middle rows | 3–6 | Extended family, close friends |
| Back rows | 7+ | General guests |
| Reserved signs | Row 1 | "Reserved for Family of the Bride/Groom" |
| Mobility accessible | Aisle seats, front | Guests with mobility needs |
