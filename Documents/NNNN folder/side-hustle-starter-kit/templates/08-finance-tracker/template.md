# Freelance Finance Tracker

Track every dollar in and out. Know your real income, tax liability, and profitability every month.

## How to Use

1. Log income and expenses weekly (Sunday evening works well)
2. Use the monthly summary to know your tax liability
3. Move money to your tax account every time you get paid
4. Review quarterly to spot trends

## Tax Planning

### Estimated Quarterly Taxes (US Self-Employed)

US self-employed individuals pay estimated taxes 4x per year:

| Quarter | Period Covered | Due Date |
|---------|---------------|----------|
| Q1 | Jan 1 – Mar 31 | April 15 |
| Q2 | Apr 1 – May 31 | June 15 |
| Q3 | Jun 1 – Aug 31 | September 15 |
| Q4 | Sep 1 – Dec 31 | January 15 (next year) |

**Tax rate guidance for self-employed:**
- Self-employment tax (Social Security + Medicare): ~15.3%
- Federal income tax: 10–37% depending on income
- State income tax: 0–13% depending on state
- **Safe bet: Set aside 25–30% of every payment**

### Tax-Deductible Business Expenses

Keep receipts for everything. Common deductions:
- Home office (% of rent/mortgage/utilities)
- Computer and equipment (100% if used for business)
- Software subscriptions
- Business-related phone/internet (% of bill)
- Professional development (courses, books, conferences)
- Health insurance premiums (if self-employed)
- Retirement contributions (SEP-IRA, Solo 401k)
- Vehicle mileage for business travel ($0.67/mile in 2024)
- Business banking fees
- Accounting/legal fees

## Annual Tax Prep Checklist

**Gather by January 31:**
- [ ] 1099-NEC forms from any client who paid you $600+ (US)
- [ ] All income records (this tracker!)
- [ ] All expense receipts organized by category
- [ ] Home office measurements (sq ft)
- [ ] Mileage log
- [ ] Retirement contribution records

**Consider:**
- [ ] Maximizing retirement contributions before tax deadline
- [ ] Hiring an accountant (their fee is tax-deductible!)
