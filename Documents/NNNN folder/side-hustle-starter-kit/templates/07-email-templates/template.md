# Email Swipe File for Freelancers

10 battle-tested email templates for every stage of the client relationship.
Replace [BRACKETS] with your details. Keep edits minimal — these are proven to work.

---

## 1. Cold Outreach (First Contact)

**Subject:** Quick question about [their company/project]

> Hi [First Name],
>
> I came across [their company/website/work] and noticed [specific observation — e.g., "your blog content is great but you're posting inconsistently"].
>
> I'm a [your role] who helps [type of client] [achieve specific result]. [One-sentence proof: e.g., "I recently helped a SaaS company increase their email open rates by 34% in 60 days."]
>
> Would it make sense to have a quick 15-minute call to see if I could help you [achieve similar result]?
>
> Either way, no pressure — I just thought there might be a fit.
>
> Best,
> [Your Name]
> [yourwebsite.com] | [LinkedIn URL]

*Best practices: Personalize the first sentence for every email. Send Tuesday–Thursday, 8–10am. Follow up once after 5 days.*

## 2. Follow-Up After No Response

**Subject:** Re: Quick question about [their company]

> Hi [First Name],
>
> Just wanted to bump this to the top of your inbox in case it got buried.
>
> [Restate offer in one line: "I help [client type] with [service] — happy to share a few ideas specific to your situation at no charge."]
>
> If the timing isn't right, no worries at all. Just let me know and I won't follow up again.
>
> Best,
> [Your Name]

*Send this exactly 5 business days after the first email. After two no-responses, move on.*

## 3. Discovery Call Confirmation

**Subject:** Our call on [Day, Date] at [Time] — here's what to expect

> Hi [First Name],
>
> Looking forward to our call on [Day, Date] at [Time] [timezone].
>
> [Call link: Zoom/Google Meet URL]
>
> To make the most of our 30 minutes, it would help to know:
> 1. What's your biggest challenge with [relevant area] right now?
> 2. What does success look like in 6 months?
>
> Feel free to reply with answers before the call, or we can chat through them live.
>
> See you then!
>
> [Your Name]

## 4. Proposal / Quote Email

**Subject:** [Project Name] — Proposal for [Client Company]

> Hi [First Name],
>
> Thank you for taking the time to chat — it was great to learn more about [project/challenge they shared].
>
> Based on our conversation, here's my proposed approach:
>
> **Project:** [Project Name]
> **Scope:** [Brief 2–3 line description of what you'll do]
> **Timeline:** [X weeks, starting [date]]
> **Investment:** $[amount]
>
> [Optional: "I've attached a detailed proposal PDF."]
>
> This price includes [list what's included] and [number] rounds of revisions. [Any notable exclusions.]
>
> To move forward, I just need a signed contract and 50% deposit, and I can start on [date].
>
> Do you have any questions, or would you like to proceed?
>
> Best,
> [Your Name]

## 7. Asking for a Testimonial

**Subject:** Quick favor — would you share your thoughts?

> Hi [First Name],
>
> I hope [result from the project] is going well!
>
> I'm building out my portfolio and would love a brief testimonial from you if you've been happy with the work.
>
> It doesn't need to be long — even 2–3 sentences about [what specifically they can speak to] would be incredibly helpful.
>
> You can reply here or leave a Google/LinkedIn review if you prefer.
>
> [Optional: Link to Google review page or LinkedIn recommendation request]
>
> No pressure at all — I just wanted to ask!
>
> Thanks,
> [Your Name]

## 8. Checking In (Past Client Nurture)

**Subject:** Hey [First Name] — quick check-in

> Hi [First Name],
>
> I was thinking about the work we did on [project] and wanted to check in on how things are going.
>
> [Optional: "I noticed [something relevant about their business/industry] and thought it might be helpful."]
>
> Are there any new challenges I could help with? I'm currently [availability update — e.g., "taking on 2 new clients in Q2"].
>
> No pressure at all — just wanted to stay in touch.
>
> Best,
> [Your Name]

*Send this to past clients every 3–4 months. Warm leads are easier than cold outreach.*

## 9. Handling a Price Objection

**Subject:** Re: Proposal — addressing your budget concerns

> Hi [First Name],
>
> Thanks for being straightforward about budget — I really appreciate it.
>
> I hear you on the investment level. Let me offer a few options:
>
> **Option A — Reduced scope:** We could start with [smaller version of project] at $[lower price]. This gets you [core result] now, and we can expand later.
>
> **Option B — Payment plan:** I'm happy to split the $[full price] into [X] monthly payments of $[amount each].
>
> **Option C — Prioritized deliverables:** Which parts of the project are most critical? We could focus there first and phase the rest.
>
> Which option makes the most sense for you?
>
> Best,
> [Your Name]

---

## 10. Saying No (Politely Declining)

**Subject:** Re: [Project Name]

> Hi [First Name],
>
> Thank you so much for thinking of me for this project — I can tell it's an exciting opportunity.
>
> After looking at my current commitments, I don't think I can give it the attention it deserves right now.
>
> I'd be happy to refer you to [colleague's name / "a colleague who specializes in this area"] if that would be helpful.
>
> I hope we can work together in the future.
>
> Best,
> [Your Name]

*Always leave the door open. "No for now" often becomes "yes" 6 months later.*
