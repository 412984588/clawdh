## Step 3: Calculate Your Minimum Hourly Rate

```
Minimum Hourly Rate = Annual Income Target ÷ Annual Billable Hours

Example:
  Annual income target: $72,000
  Annual billable hours: 1,344
  Minimum rate: $72,000 ÷ 1,344 = $53.57/hr
```

## Step 4: Add Your Market Rate Adjustment

Your minimum rate is the floor. Your actual rate should reflect your:

| Factor | Adjustment |
|--------|-----------|
| Years of experience (0–2 yrs) | No adjustment |
| Years of experience (3–5 yrs) | +20–40% |
| Years of experience (6+ yrs) | +50–100% |
| Niche specialization | +20–50% |
| Proven results / portfolio | +20–30% |
| Urgency / rush work | +25–50% |

## The "Value-Based Pricing" Shift

Instead of charging for your time, charge for results:

| Old thinking | New thinking |
|--------------|-------------|
| "I'll charge $50/hr for 10 hours = $500" | "This website redesign will increase their sales by $5,000/year — I'll charge $2,000" |
| "I spent 3 hours on this logo" | "This logo will represent their brand for 10+ years — it's worth $1,500" |

**Rule of thumb:** Charge 10–20% of the value you create for the client.
