# RelayOps — Demo Script v2

Upgraded public-facing demo script built for two sales contexts: a fast 3-minute walkthrough and a deeper 10-minute operational demo.

---

## 3-Minute Quick Demo

### Step 1 — Partner submits the ticket

**Goal:** Show that RelayOps starts with structured intake, not a vague service request.

**Talk track**
- “The partner starts by turning a messy client request into a standardized job brief.”
- “Instead of email back-and-forth, the workflow captures source files, success criteria, and delivery context upfront.”
- “That means operations receives a job it can actually execute.”

**What to point at**
- Intake summary
- Acceptance criteria
- Attached source files

**FAQ**
- “Do we need to train our team?”  
  The intake is designed to be lightweight. Partners provide business context, not technical implementation detail.

### Step 2 — Admin scopes and prices

**Goal:** Explain how commercial clarity is established before work starts.

**Talk track**
- “Ops reviews the request, locks scope, and prices the work before it enters fulfillment.”
- “This is where RelayOps converts a request into an executable delivery package.”
- “The important part is predictability: scope, turnaround, and pricing are all explicit.”

**What to point at**
- Pricing tier
- SLA
- Worker handoff package

**FAQ**
- “How do you prevent scope creep?”  
  Scope is locked before execution. Any change request is handled as a scoped revision or a new job.

### Step 3 — Worker executes the job

**Goal:** Show that fulfillment is driven by a fixed spec.

**Talk track**
- “The worker sees the scoped brief, the normalization rules, and the delivery checklist in one place.”
- “That reduces interpretation risk and makes handoff quality much more consistent.”
- “The operator is not guessing what ‘done’ means.”

**What to point at**
- Progress snapshot
- Validation notes
- Deliverable package

**FAQ**
- “Is this manual or automated?”  
  It is workflow-guided operations. Automation can support the process, but quality control is tied to explicit review checkpoints.

### Step 4 — Partner reviews delivery

**Goal:** Close on trust, proof, and acceptance.

**Talk track**
- “The partner reviews the cleaned output against the same criteria submitted at intake.”
- “Approval closes the job; revision sends targeted feedback back through the same workflow.”
- “That makes acceptance auditable instead of subjective.”

**What to point at**
- Delivery summary
- Review checklist
- Approve / revise decision

**FAQ**
- “What happens if the output misses the mark?”  
  The revision loop stays attached to the original job so feedback, ownership, and final acceptance remain visible.

### Quick-demo close

**Close line**
- “That is the full RelayOps operating model: structured intake, scoped pricing, controlled fulfillment, and reviewable delivery.”

## 10-Minute Deep Demo

### 1. Frame the problem

**Objective**
- Establish why agencies struggle with CRM cleanup and migration prep in the first place.

**Talk track**
- “Most agencies do not lose deals because they cannot strategize. They lose margin because cleanup and migration work becomes messy, manual, and difficult to standardize.”
- “RelayOps exists to make that operational layer reliable enough to sell.”

**FAQ**
- “Who is this best for?”  
  RevOps agencies, implementation partners, and service teams that repeatedly encounter cleanup, normalization, or migration prep work.

### 2. Partner intake walkthrough

**Objective**
- Show how the workflow captures enough detail without overcomplicating the handoff.

**Talk track**
- “The intake is opinionated on purpose. It forces the request into a usable structure.”
- “That reduces ambiguity before the job ever reaches operations.”

**Demo cues**
- Show the brief summary
- Show the acceptance criteria
- Show the file list

**FAQ**
- “Can partners attach special instructions?”  
  Yes. The brief supports contextual notes, expected output definitions, and acceptance criteria.

### 3. Admin scoping and pricing walkthrough

**Objective**
- Demonstrate that pricing is tied to scope and operational reality.

**Talk track**
- “Admin is where the system protects both delivery quality and gross margin.”
- “The team validates fit, applies a pricing tier, and clarifies the SLA before the work enters execution.”

**Demo cues**
- Highlight pricing tier
- Highlight queued status
- Highlight worker handoff notes

**FAQ**
- “Can pricing be customized?”  
  Yes. The model supports tiered pricing with scope-based adjustment before fulfillment starts.

### 4. Worker execution walkthrough

**Objective**
- Make the fulfillment layer feel disciplined and repeatable.

**Talk track**
- “The worker console is not just a task list. It is a controlled delivery environment.”
- “The operator sees the task definition, validation context, and deliverable expectations in one place.”
- “That reduces variance across projects and team members.”

**Demo cues**
- Progress signal
- Exception notes
- Deliverable packaging

**FAQ**
- “How do you handle edge cases?”  
  Exceptions are logged visibly so ambiguous rows or mapping problems do not disappear into silent assumptions.

### 5. Delivery review walkthrough

**Objective**
- Show how the final stage creates trust with the partner.

**Talk track**
- “The review step is where operational trust compounds.”
- “The partner receives the output, the summary, and a checklist tied back to the original ask.”
- “Approval and revision both live in the same workflow, so there is no break in accountability.”

**Demo cues**
- Delivery summary
- Acceptance checklist
- Approval decision

**FAQ**
- “Can we track revision history?”  
  Yes. Revision requests stay attached to the job record, which preserves a clean delivery timeline.

### 6. Deep-demo close

**Close line**
- “RelayOps is not selling generic cleanup labor. It is selling a controlled service workflow agencies can rely on when data quality work becomes business-critical.”

## FAQ Cheat Sheet

### “Is this replacing our internal ops team?”

No. It is designed to absorb repetitive, scopeable data operations work so the internal team can stay focused on strategy, client management, and higher-value implementation decisions.

### “What kinds of work fit best?”

CRM deduplication, field normalization, import prep, migration staging, compliance-ready data organization, and structured delivery packages around recurring data operations tasks.

### “Why show a mock UI instead of the live dashboard?”

The public demo explains the workflow without exposing internal product surfaces. It keeps the story lightweight, reliable, and safe to share externally.

### “What should the CTA be at the end?”

Move directly to `/request-access` and frame it as the next step for agencies that want to operationalize this workflow in production.
